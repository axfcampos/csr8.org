  • Technical Awards
  • B. Ramakrishna Rau Award
    The IEEE made available a prestigious award to professionals who made an outstanding, innovative contribution or contributions to the computer microarchitecture community. If you have also contributed to the community through teaching, mentoring, or community service it will be a plus! The award consist of a certificate, a $2,000 honorarium, and will be invited to present a paper and/or presentation at the ACM/IEEE International Symposium on Microarchitecture.
  • Computer Entrepreneur Award
    If you are responsible for the growth of some segment of the computer industry you are eligible to apply to the distinguished Computer Entrepreneur Award . The efforts must have taken place over fifteen years earlier, and the industry effects must be generally and openly visible. The deadline is on the 15th October.
  • Computer Pioneer Award
    The Computer Pioneer Award aims to recognize and honor the efforts of people who have greatly contributed to the concepts and development of the computer field in the last fifteen years. You are invited to nominate a colleague you think is eligible to receive this award. The nomination deadline is on the 15 October 2013. The recognition will be engraved on a silver medal specially struck for the Society.
  • Eckert-Mauchly Award
    Have you made outstanding contributions to the field of computer and digital systems architecture? If so, you are eligible to apply to receive the Eckert-Mauchly Award which consists of a certificate and $5,000 offered by ACM and Computer Society. The award will be presented at the prestigious conference ISCA 2013. The deadline is on 30 March 2014.

  • Harlan D. Mills Award
    IEEE has made available this award to honour outstanding researchers and professionals who has substantially contributed towards information sciences, specially on the practice of software engineering through the application of sound theory. The winner of this award will receive a $3,000 honorarium, museum-quality memento, and possibly be invited to be a speaker in the International Conference on Software Engineering (ICSE).
  • Harry H. Goode Memorial Award
    This award aims to honour the achievements in the information processing field. If you have made outstanding contributions on the theory, design or technique of great significance you can apply to this award, and if you win, you will receive a bronze medal and $2,000 from the Computer Society. The deadline is on October 15th.
  • Hans Karlsson Standards Award
    A plaque and $2,000 will be awarded to present of past participants in the IEEE CS Standard activities, who has presented dedication, outstanding skills, worked towards the development or promotion of standards in the computer industry. The deadline to apply is on the 15th October.
  • Ken Kennedy Award
    The ACM and the IEEE Computer Society is offering a certificate and $5,000 honorarium to outstanding contributions to programmability or productivity in high-performance computing together with significant community service or mentoring contributions. The deadline to apply is on the 1st July.

  • Seymour Cray Computer Engineering Award
    A crystal memento, illuminated certificate, and $10,000 honorarium are awarded to recognize innovative contributions to high performance computing systems. The deadline to apply is on the 1st July.
  • Sidney Fernbach Award
    The IEEE is offering a certificate and $2,000 are awarded for outstanding contributions in the application of high performance computers using innovative approaches. The deadline to apply is on the 1st July.
  • Technical Achievement Award
    A certificate and $2,000 honorarium being offered to honour outstanding and innovative contributions in the fields of information science and engineering or computer technology. In case you have made significant contributions within the past ten, and no more than fifteen years, you are eligible to apply by the 15th October.
  • W. Wallace McDowell Award
    The IEEE Computer Society wants to present a certificate of $2,000 honorarium for outstanding recent theoretical, design, educational, practical, or any other innovative contributions that is of interest of the society. You can nominate yourself to this award by 15th October.
